Welcome to Module 3 of our Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Textbook on AI-robot integration using NVIDIA Isaac. This module introduces you to advanced perception, navigation, and training for humanoid robots using NVIDIA Isaac, bridging AI models with physical robot behavior.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:
- Explain the role of NVIDIA Isaac in Physical AI
- Understand photorealistic simulation and synthetic data
- Describe visual SLAM and humanoid navigation concepts

## Target Audience

This module is designed for:
- Students familiar with ROS 2 and simulation concepts
- AI learners moving toward embodied intelligence

## Prerequisites

This module assumes you have:
- Understanding of ROS 2 concepts (covered in [Module 1: The Robotic Nervous System](../module-1/intro.md))
- Knowledge of simulation concepts (covered in [Module 2: The Digital Twin](../module-2/intro.md))
- Basic understanding of robotics fundamentals

## Structure

This module consists of three chapters that progressively build your understanding of AI-robot integration:

1. [Isaac Sim Overview](./chapter-1-isaac-sim-overview.md) - Learn about photorealistic simulation and synthetic data generation
2. [Isaac ROS Perception](./chapter-2-isaac-ros-perception.md) - Understand hardware-accelerated perception and visual SLAM
3. [Navigation Concepts with Nav2](./chapter-3-nav2-humanoid-navigation.md) - Discover humanoid navigation fundamentals

The content focuses on conceptual understanding without requiring complex installation procedures or advanced setup, building on your existing ROS 2 and simulation knowledge.

## Learning Exercises

To reinforce your understanding of the concepts covered in this module, consider the following exercises:

1. **Concept Mapping Exercise**: Create a diagram showing how Isaac Sim, Isaac ROS, and Nav2 work together to form a complete AI-robot brain system for humanoid robots.

2. **Application Scenario**: Think of a specific humanoid robot application (e.g., home assistant, industrial inspection, healthcare companion) and describe how the three components of NVIDIA Isaac would contribute to its functionality.

3. **Comparison Analysis**: Compare and contrast the advantages of hardware-accelerated perception in Isaac ROS versus traditional CPU-based approaches for humanoid robot applications.

4. **Navigation Challenge**: Identify three specific challenges that humanoid robots face in navigation that are different from wheeled robots, and explain how Nav2 addresses these challenges.
